Product thesis

Athena already stores useful business facts across Store Pulse, Daily Operations, Cash Controls, Procurement, SKU Activity, Transactions, Services, online orders, and storefront analytics. Reports should compose those facts into one balanced business read, explain material changes, disclose incomplete evidence, and preserve a path back to every supporting record.

Reports interprets and routes. Procurement, Cash Controls, Transactions, Product editing, SKU Activity, Daily Operations, and Terminal health continue to own corrective action.

Approved decisions

Overview

Balanced business pulse

Lead with unified net sales, merchandise gross profit, units sold, current inventory value, and attention signals.

Time

Week to date

Compare with the same elapsed portion of the prior week in the store's operating timezone.

Revenue

Unified, with explicit lanes

Combine POS, storefront, products, and services at the top line; keep merchandise and service profitability distinct.

Cost

Moving weighted average

Receipts update future SKU cost, sales snapshot cost, and missing cost remains visible without blocking trade.

Items

SKU first

SKU is authoritative for stock, cost, velocity, and replenishment; products and categories roll up from SKU facts.

Refunds

Dual basis

Money moves in the refund period, while adjusted SKU performance attributes the outcome to the original sale.

Evidence

Every aggregate drills down

SKU performance links to every attached transaction and then to full SKU Activity for non-sale evidence.

Protection

Full-admin only

Route and backend reads protect revenue, cost, margin, valuation, and evidence from manager elevation and POS-only access.

Metric contract

Unified net sales

Recognized product and service sales across POS and storefront, less refunds recognized in the selected period, without duplicate settlement paths.

Merchandise gross profit

Eligible merchandise net sales less immutable recognized merchandise cost. Service revenue is excluded from this profit lane.

Cost coverage

Merchandise revenue with known immutable cost divided by total eligible merchandise revenue, with the uncovered amount visible.

Current inventory value

Current on-hand units multiplied by trustworthy weighted-average SKU cost, with uncosted units disclosed.

Sales velocity

Net recognized units per active day over a disclosed lookback window.

Estimated days of cover

Current sellable units divided by disclosed daily velocity; unavailable when history is insufficient.

Purchase commitment

Remaining ordered quantity and value for purchase orders not fully received or cancelled, split between planned and inbound state.

Refund activity

Financial refund value processed during the period, regardless of original sale date.

Adjusted SKU performance

Item outcome with later refunds, returns, and corrections attributed to the original sale for performance analysis.

Inventory consumed

SKU quantity and item cost recorded by expense or service usage; never labeled as complete operating expense.

Requirements

Expand each group to review the complete approved behavioral contract.

Workspace and access · R1-R12
  1. R1. Scope Reports to the currently selected store.
  2. R2. Require full-admin access at route and backend read boundaries.
  3. R3. Do not expose Reports through manager elevation or pos_only access.
  4. R4. Default to week to date in the store operating timezone.
  5. R5. Compare with the same elapsed portion of the prior week.
  6. R6. Support today, prior week, trailing 30 days, and custom ranges.
  7. R7. Show selected range, comparison range, refresh time, and partial days.
  8. R8. Present a balanced money-and-inventory pulse.
  9. R9. Lead with net sales, merchandise profit, units, inventory value, and attention.
  10. R10. Move storefront engagement analytics under a secondary Storefront view.
  11. R11. Reuse Athena workspace metric and drill-down patterns.
  12. R12. Format all amounts with the active store currency.
Unified revenue and profitability · R13-R22
  1. R13. Include completed POS products, POS services, storefront orders, and recognized service-case revenue.
  2. R14. Prevent duplicate counting across settlement and source records.
  3. R15. Show product, service, POS, and storefront contributions beneath the total.
  4. R16. Give gross sales, discounts, refunds, and net sales one documented meaning.
  5. R17. Calculate merchandise COGS from immutable sale-line cost.
  6. R18. Define merchandise gross profit as eligible net sales less recognized COGS.
  7. R19. Calculate margin only on merchandise revenue with known cost.
  8. R20. Show cost coverage beside every profit or margin view.
  9. R21. Keep service revenue separate from merchandise profit until service cost exists.
  10. R22. Do not present combined product-and-service gross profit while service delivery cost is unknown.
SKU-first item performance · R23-R30
  1. R23. Use SKU as the authoritative item-performance level.
  2. R24. Derive product and category totals from SKU facts.
  3. R25. Show units, returns, net movement, sales, cost, profit, margin, stock, value, velocity, cover, and comparison where trustworthy.
  4. R26. Support finding winners, slow movers, low-cover winners, weak-margin volume, and trapped capital.
  5. R27. Distinguish on-hand inventory from sellable availability.
  6. R28. Withhold period inventory metrics when opening, inbound, or outbound evidence is incomplete.
  7. R29. Disclose the velocity window behind estimated cover.
  8. R30. Do not label time since movement or sale as true stock age.
Highlight and route · R31-R38
  1. R31. Highlight deterministic integrity, money, inventory, and operational exceptions.
  2. R32. Explain the change, affected value, rule, and completeness limitation.
  3. R33. Provide one route to the workflow that owns resolution.
  4. R34. Route stock-cover and inbound issues to Procurement.
  5. R35. Route register variance to Cash Controls or session evidence.
  6. R36. Route refund, discount, void, and correction signals to transaction evidence.
  7. R37. Route missing cost to SKU editing without blocking sales.
  8. R38. Route uncertain POS synchronization to Terminal health.
Evidence and drill-down · R39-R46
  1. R39. Preserve a path from every aggregate to supporting records.
  2. R40. Show all selected-period transactions attached to a trusted SKU with financial and adjustment evidence.
  3. R41. Route to full SKU Activity for non-sale movement and provenance.
  4. R42. State when results are truncated, omitted, stale, or awaiting POS synchronization.
  5. R43. Keep missing cost distinct from legitimate zero cost.
  6. R44. Never block trade because cost is missing.
  7. R45. Never substitute current cost for unknown historical sale cost.
  8. R46. Use Daily Close completeness evidence to inform trust for closed days.
Cost and inventory valuation · R47-R52
  1. R47. Maintain moving weighted-average cost at SKU level.
  2. R48. Update future weighted-average cost from eligible on-hand cost and costed receipts.
  3. R49. Snapshot applicable cost on every recognized merchandise sale line.
  4. R50. Reintroduce sellable returns at the original known sale cost.
  5. R51. Apply manual cost corrections prospectively unless an explicit auditable backfill occurs.
  6. R52. Keep unknown-cost inventory visibly uncosted until a trustworthy basis exists.
Refund and return semantics · R53-R58
  1. R53. Recognize financial refunds when they occur.
  2. R54. Keep refund activity visible in the refund period.
  3. R55. Attribute adjusted SKU outcome to the original sale.
  4. R56. Distinguish refund, return, restock, exchange, void, and correction.
  5. R57. Reverse COGS only when inventory disposition supports it.
  6. R58. Keep non-restocked merchandise represented as loss, expense, or adjustment.
Expense and service boundaries · R59-R63
  1. R59. Label existing expense transactions as inventory expense or inventory consumed.
  2. R60. Do not describe SKU expense transactions as complete operating expenses.
  3. R61. Do not claim net profit, cash flow, payroll, rent, utilities, or unrecorded overhead.
  4. R62. Add service profitability later without redefining merchandise metrics.
  5. R63. Keep historical service cost unknown unless explicitly and audibly backfilled.

Acceptance examples

  1. Wednesday afternoon compares Monday through the current Wednesday time with the equivalent prior-week window and identifies Wednesday as partial.
  2. A mixed POS product-and-service transaction, its service case, and a storefront order each contribute recognized revenue exactly once.
  3. A period with 90% costed merchandise revenue shows profit only for eligible revenue, 90% coverage, and the uncovered amount.
  4. Product variants with different stock, cost, and velocity remain separate SKU rows while their product rollup reconciles exactly.
  5. A trusted SKU with POS and storefront sales exposes every attached transaction and a route to its complete SKU Activity history.
  6. Ten units at GHS 20 plus ten received at GHS 30 produce a future weighted-average cost of GHS 25 without rewriting older sales.
  7. A Week 2 refund for a Week 1 sale appears in Week 2 money movement, adjusts original SKU performance, and increases inventory only when the item is restocked.
  8. A low-cover fast mover with no inbound PO explains the risk and links to Procurement without creating a purchase order in Reports.
  9. Delayed terminal synchronization marks affected values incomplete and links to terminal evidence rather than presenting them as final.
  10. SKU inventory consumed through an expense workflow is labeled inventory consumed; Reports does not present operating or net profit.

Product boundaries

Inside this product

  • Store-level business performance
  • Unified recognized revenue
  • Merchandise profit with coverage
  • SKU movement, stock, value, and evidence
  • Purchase commitments and incoming cover
  • Deterministic signals and workflow routes
  • Freshness and completeness disclosure

Outside this product

  • Organization-wide rollups
  • POS-only or manager-elevation access
  • Mutation and operational work queues
  • General ledger and accounting net profit
  • Service profit before delivery cost exists
  • FIFO, true stock age, and lot analysis
  • Opaque forecasting and configurable targets
Truth over availability. Missing cost, incomplete movement history, or delayed POS data withholds or qualifies affected metrics. Reports never fills gaps with current values presented as historical fact.
