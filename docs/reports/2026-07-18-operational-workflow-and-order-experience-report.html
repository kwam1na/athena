Executive Summary

This candidate makes daily operations easier to scan and act on, keeps POS expense work focused on the signed-in cashier, and gives order, refund, and return workflows a consistent workspace hierarchy.

It also tightens two backend boundaries: organization members are projected to their declared public shape through an indexed query, and sync-review completeness is based on conflicted events linked to resolved conflicts rather than arbitrary resolved history.

Mental Model

Daily Operations is a routing and visibility surface. Cash Controls and Open Work remain the authoritative places for cash and review resolution. The UI changes make that routing clearer while keeping existing commands, closeout policy, and order state transitions intact.

  • Daily Opening: visible acknowledgement progress and bulk acknowledgement controls, while Start Day remains gated.
  • Daily Close: clearer register-specific status and a flatter review rail, with details still available on demand.
  • Orders: payment method and amount are co-located; refund and return surfaces keep their existing commands but present their choices more clearly.
  • POS expenses: cashier context moves into the header and the cart rail owns the single visible total.

Failure Boundaries

The change does not broaden public APIs or alter drawer, closeout, refund, return, or order-transition policy. The shared-demo owner deliberately uses store@osustudio.com, and its validator now asserts that explicit product identity rather than applying a generic synthetic-email rule. Sync lookup fan-out is bounded per review limit, so unusually large conflict histories remain an operational scale boundary rather than an unbounded read.
