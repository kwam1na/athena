7. The positioning gate passes only at four or five passing sessions. Zero through three passing sessions reopens R2, R5, R7, and the corresponding key decisions before final copy or media approval.

## Eligibility and deliberate sample mix

Every valid participant must:

- be the owner or primary operator accountable for day-to-day decisions in a product-heavy small or medium business;
- regularly sell physical products and coordinate at least one employee or recurring worker;
- personally review sales and inventory information when deciding what to do next;
- not be an Athena employee, contractor, close project collaborator, existing design reviewer, or a person previously briefed on this positioning;
- be able to complete the session without another participant supplying answers.

Before recruitment, the product owner must assign each slot to a deliberate mix cell. Across the five valid sessions the frozen mix must include:

- at least two operators with one to five employees;
- at least one operator with more than five employees;
- at least two operators who currently reconstruct business standing from notebooks, physical receipts, memory, or manually combined records;
- at least one operator selling through both an in-person and an online channel.

A participant may satisfy more than one mix cell. The product owner may add a sector or maturity constraint before the freeze, but may not add one after seeing responses.

## Frozen stimulus

The facilitator must show one stimulus at a time and must not explain it.

1. **Problem stimulus:** the exact problem statement at the frozen stimulus revision, describing business evidence split across notebooks, physical receipts, and memory, and the delayed decisions caused by reconstruction.
2. **Entry-promise stimulus:** `Athena helps you have visibility into your day's sales.`
3. **Connected-record stimulus:** the exact frozen explanation and product sequence for sales standing → Athena-recorded history → product drivers → current stock pressure → an owner-led restocking decision.

The connected-record stimulus must explicitly say that history accumulates in Athena through use. It must not imply that Athena automatically reconstructs old notebooks or receipts, forecasts the future, or automatically chooses what to reorder.

## Fixed prompt order

Record the complete answer after each prompt before moving on.

1. “In your own words, who is this for?”
2. “What problem do you think this is trying to solve?”
3. “What does Athena help the owner do first?”
4. “What becomes possible after the business has used Athena over time?”
5. “Why is the connected record more useful than seeing only today's sales total?”
6. “Who decides what to restock, and what information supports that decision?”
7. “What, if anything, is unclear or sounds like more than the product can do?”

After prompt 7 and only after the response is saved, the facilitator may ask neutral follow-ups such as “What makes you say that?” Follow-up answers are qualitative context and do not repair a failed scored answer.

## Frozen scoring rubric

Use only what the participant says without help. Exact wording is not required.

| Outcome | Pass | Fail | Passing example | Failing example |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Problem | Identifies fragmented business evidence or the manual reconstruction of business standing, plus the resulting uncertainty or delayed decisions. | Describes only a generic need for software, advertising, accounting, or a faster checkout. | “The owner has sales information in receipts and memory and loses time piecing it together before deciding.” | “It gives a shop a more modern website.” |
| Athena's role | Identifies visibility into the day's sales as the entry value and understands that Athena keeps the operating record as the business uses it. | Says Athena imports or reconstructs all historical paper evidence automatically, or only describes a payment terminal. | “It shows how the day is going and keeps that activity available for later comparison.” | “It scans every old receipt and predicts next month's sales.” |
| Connected-record difference | Explains that sales history stays connected to product movement and current stock context so the owner can make a better-informed restocking decision. Keeps the owner as decision-maker. | Merely repeats “more data,” claims Athena automatically generates a reorder decision from sales, or cannot name a practical difference from a daily total. | “The owner can see what sold, compare it with prior activity, and check current stock before deciding what to order.” | “Athena automatically orders whatever sold most.” |

Ambiguous answers fail the affected outcome. Record uncertainty in the notes; do not infer the intended answer.
