Reports Workspace

How Athena turns verified projections into trusted operator-facing reports

Executive Summary

Athena now has a full-admin Reports workspace for money, item movement, inventory exposure, and Storefront engagement. The old /analytics route is intentionally removed; Storefront analytics now lives at /reports/storefront.

Financial views do not reconstruct accounting meaning in React. Server-owned, store-schedule-aware read models expose explicit trust, coverage, currency, and unavailable states.

Mental Model

Think of projections as verified source ledgers and a Reports workspace epoch as a published edition. Preset reports build an isolated watermark-bound epoch and atomically switch the store read pointer. A custom request freezes its own sources, derives range families, builds a separate custom presentation epoch, and remains materializing until that epoch is active.

  • Operational facts feed daily, inventory, intraday, and Daily Close trust projections.
  • Store-schedule period authority chooses the exact operating dates and same-elapsed cutoff.
  • Resumable materializers build Overview, Items, Inventory, facets, classifications, and trust into an isolated epoch.
  • Authorized public DTOs require full-admin store access and the active matching epoch.
  • Route loaders and views render the answer, its coverage, and its limiting reason; they do not reconstruct financial meaning.

Layer-by-Layer Flow

  • Facts → projections: immutable reporting evidence becomes store-day, SKU-day, current inventory, intraday checkpoints, and trust summaries. Invalid density blocks explicitly.
  • Projections → preset epoch: bounded replay-safe pages build every workspace family. Duplicate pages no-op; blocked intraday readiness prevents activation without polling forever.
  • Custom request → custom epoch: compatible generations are frozen, store/SKU/derive phases resume, and the UI remains Preparing reports until activation.
  • Epoch → public DTO: authorization, schedule, generation, watermark, filter, sort, and cursor context are rechecked. A stale or mismatched cursor fails instead of mixing editions.
  • DTO → UI: Overview, Items, detail, and Inventory show complete, partial, mixed-currency, stale, materializing, pre-cutover, or unavailable states. Storefront uses its independent live timeframe.

Before and After

Before, /analytics was a Storefront engagement page and the reporting foundation lacked a safe composite UI boundary. After, /reports owns Overview, Items, SKU detail, Inventory, and Storefront, with financial periods resolved from store operating schedules and custom ranges processed asynchronously.

Trust and Failure Boundaries

  • Missing cost preserves revenue but limits profit and valuation coverage.
  • Mixed currencies withhold combined money instead of attaching the wrong symbol.
  • Current inventory and selected-period movement remain separate authorities.
  • Pagination is bound to store, authorization, period, filter, sort, contracts, watermarks, and active epoch.
  • Intraday evidence fails closed above the exact remainder bound and exposes a recoverable blocked state.
  • Unsupported evidence destinations remain unavailable; Reports invents no deep links.

Custom Ranges

Custom ranges freeze compatible source generations, resume through store, SKU, and derive phases, then build a presentation epoch. The UI says Preparing reports until the verified epoch is active; it never treats an unfinished range as empty or current.

What Did Not Change

  • Storefront engagement keeps its independent live timeframe.
  • No organization rollups, store comparison, manager elevation, or pos_only access were added.
  • Reports does not mutate operational records or repair data.
  • Service profitability and unsupported service/payment detail routes remain unavailable.

Next-Time Guidance

  • Add a report metric at the projection/read-model boundary, not in React.
  • Extend every new family through isolated epoch materialization, verification, activation, and cursor context.
  • Use exact indexed evidence boundaries before applying scan limits.
  • Model blocked recovery explicitly; do not poll permanently.
  • Keep route search ownership layered: shared period context may cross tabs; a validated custom run ID may cross financial tabs only; Storefront drops it; cursor/filter/sort never cross views.

Quiz: Pass Required

You must score at least 8 out of 10 to pass.

1. What is the authoritative UI boundary for financial Reports?
Reports reads verified, server-shaped DTOs from the active workspace epoch.
2. Why build a new workspace epoch before switching readers?
Isolation plus atomic activation keeps the prior complete edition visible during rebuild.
3. What happens when cost evidence is missing?
Unknown cost is a limitation, not zero and not a reason to suppress trusted revenue.
4. What validation best proves the cross-layer Reports contract?
The contract spans projections, epochs, public DTOs, routes, and presentation, so it needs cross-layer tests and compile/build proof.
5. What state may safely cross financial Reports tabs?
View-owned cursor/filter/sort state is cleared; custom run ID is limited to financial tabs and is dropped for Storefront.
6. Why is inventory split into exposure and movement?
Current position is as-of now; movement belongs to the selected period.
7. What happens when an intraday remainder exceeds the evidence bound?
Exactness fails closed and recovery is explicit and bounded.
8. When is a custom report shown as ready?
Materializing is distinct from ready, preventing false empty results.
9. Where should a future financial metric be added?
New meaning must flow through the reporting authority and verified epoch before the UI renders it.

The requested finish line includes merge, deployment, tracking closure, and root alignment.
