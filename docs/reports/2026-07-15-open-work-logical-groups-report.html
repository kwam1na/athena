Logical Open Work Groups

One server-owned projection across Queue, Daily Operations, EOD Review, and Opening Handoff

Executive Summary

Athena previously showed one same-SKU synced-sale inventory decision in Open Work while Daily Operations, EOD Review, and Opening Handoff still counted or carried the underlying sale rows. PR #665 moves consolidation into one pure server-owned projection so every operational surface uses the same logical unit.

Raw operational work rows remain authoritative for audit, stock proof, status, and terminal transitions. The projection changes how operators count and act on those rows; it does not replace them with a persisted group entity.

Why It Mattered

Cross-surface drift made the same work look like one item in one workspace and several items elsewhere. Client-side grouping also happened after server caps and resolved members sequentially, so counts could be misleading and grouped resolution could partially progress.

The delivery makes the operator-visible unit explicit without discarding sale-level evidence. It also makes incomplete reads honest: a bounded source reports N+, exposes no actionable membership, and blocks EOD completion until complete evidence is available.

Mental Model

Think of the raw rows as receipts and the logical group as the review envelope. Operators review one envelope per store and canonical SKU, but Athena keeps every receipt inside for proof and lifecycle transitions.

Only synced-sale inventory reviews consolidate. Missing-SKU work and every unrelated work type remain source-owned singletons.

Before and After

  • Before: Open Work grouped in React after caps; other surfaces counted raw rows. After: bounded source reads feed one projection before logical counting, filtering, presentation caps, freezing, or resolving.
  • Before: the browser resolved grouped rows sequentially. After: one mutation re-derives current membership, validates every member before the first write, and commits atomically.
  • Before: EOD and Opening carried row-level work. After: EOD freezes exact complete logical membership and Opening reconstructs only that immutable evidence.
  • Before: source overflow could look exact. After: incompleteness is a lower bound with no member IDs and a single operational notice.

Resolution and Concurrency

The client submits the logical key and exact member IDs it reviewed. The mutation authenticates before broad probes, re-projects current open and in-progress work, validates shared stock proof and every source row, then compares sorted membership before any patch or event.

There are no observation tokens, signatures, expiries, or rotation rules. If anything changed, the command writes nothing and returns: This work changed. Review the refreshed group before marking it reviewed.

The card stays disabled through conflict reconciliation. A changed complete reactive DTO clears it automatically; an explicit keyed refresh also clears a recoverable same-membership conflict only after the newer complete response arrives.

Completeness, Caps, and Urgency

  • Presentation overflow and source incompleteness are separate. More than 100 complete cards is ordinary overflow; an incomplete source produces N+ and removes actionable membership.
  • Server-owned totals and per-type counts are computed before the card cap, so selected filters remain truthful even when their first card falls beyond the all-work page.
  • Logical urgency is derived independently across all members: strongest priority, any in-progress state, and oldest actionable timestamp.
  • Daily Operations remains navigation-only and emits one updating attention item when repair or work probes are incomplete.

EOD and Opening Evidence

Complete EOD Review persists a versioned logical group with stable display evidence and every frozen raw member ID. Incomplete EOD reads expose only an opaque non-actionable subject and lower-bound count; human and automation completion both fail closed.

Opening Handoff reconstructs only the prior close's frozen members. Later same-SKU work does not enter the old acknowledgement. Fully terminal frozen groups disappear, while missing or malformed frozen evidence becomes a non-bypassable integrity blocker for normal, manager-routed, and automated starts.

Legacy snapshots remain readable as persisted singletons and are never regrouped from current mutable rows.

Oversized Repair Boundary

The normal command is bounded at 50 transition rows because one group can require a row patch and an audit event per member after validation. Larger legitimate groups remain one visible logical item but move to an internal support-only repair workflow.

Repair freezes source identities and membership, validates the same source and stock-proof contracts, processes bounded batches, pauses on drift, and requires audited amendment or resume. Later distinct same-SKU sales remain separate. Each create, pause, amend, resume, and complete action is stored as its own typed append-only row plus operational events, avoiding an unbounded evidence array.
