The mental model

Store Schedule answers “when did we expect to operate?” It does not answer “did a sale financially happen?” The new model keeps those questions separate.

1 · SourcePOS transaction and lifecycle mutation
2 · CalendarOccurrence plus effective store timezone
3 · FactsImmutable money, units, item, and lineage semantics
4 · ProofIndependent census and exact reconciliation
5 · ReadCompatible projections and one active bundle

Schedule context is descriptive. Closed-day, outside-hours, and missing-hours states may affect comparisons or operational context, but never invalidate a completed sale.

Important mechanics

Source identity stays honest

Resolved pending-checkout lines may retain either their original provisional tuple or their approved catalog tuple. Both are accepted only with store, organization, pending-lineage, and catalog ownership evidence. No canonical SKU is invented when attribution evidence is absent.

Reconciliation is the activation certificate

The independent census compares identity, lifecycle, amount, quantity, date, currency, and lineage. A persisted verified certificate—not a locally recomputed subset—controls downstream completion. Convex fractional creation timestamps are valid watermarks.

The 42 orphan payment-correction events point to deleted or missing parent transactions, so their amount, currency, ownership, and settlement lineage cannot be proven. They are sealed as explicit non-facts and counted in the census hash. A correction with a present but cross-store, misbound, or temporally invalid parent remains a blocking discrepancy.

Unavailable is not zero

Legacy compatibility-shadow inventory does not count as new authority. With no authoritative inventory footprint, Athena activates an empty inventory member marked unavailable/source_incomplete. Empty sales periods, by contrast, receive explicit zero summaries only after complete POS coverage proves the range is trustworthy.

Intentional boundaries

  • Operational POS, payment, product, inventory, store, and schedule records are never purged by the reporting reset.
  • The purge script refuses production and accepts only dev or local.
  • Legacy v1 reporting facts are not preserved; this early-development migration is intentionally new-only.
  • Missing cost does not block sales. Profit and valuation remain withheld with explicit coverage.
  • Mixed or inconsistent authoritative inventory still fails closed. Only globally absent new authority can certify unavailable.
  • Production deployment, merge, and local-main alignment had not occurred when this report was rendered.
