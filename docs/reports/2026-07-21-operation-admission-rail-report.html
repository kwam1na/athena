Operation Admission Rail

This candidate moves Athena public write admission toward a platform-owned rail: operation definitions declare the write, actor adapters enforce policy, and the exported Convex mutation must prove it is wrapped before it can leave the legacy inventory.

Reviewer loop approved Focused tests passed Typecheck passed Merge gate passed

Executive Summary

The change introduces `operationAdmission`, a neutral Convex admission layer for public write mutations. The first migrated proof is `operations/openWorkInventoryReviews:resolveSyncedSaleInventoryReviewGroup`, used by the Operations Queue to resolve synced-sale inventory review groups.

The Athena-wide capability catalog now lives in `convex/platform/capabilityCatalog.ts`, not under `sharedDemo`. Shared demo remains an actor adapter that consumes the platform catalog and adds demo-specific constraints: server-owned principal, store/org clamp, restore epoch readiness, capability allow/deny, and stable denials before any domain write.

Mental Model

The rail is an entry checkpoint, not a replacement for domain rules. Admission answers: who is trying this operation, what capability is required, what scope is allowed, and whether the actor is ready to write. The domain handler still owns business invariants such as full-admin role, current work membership, repair conflicts, and inventory proof.

Public Mutation

Convex export stays a direct `mutation({ ... })`.

Wrapper

`admitPublicMutation` resolves admission before handler execution.

Definition

Operation declares capability, scope, readiness, effects, and actor coverage.

Adapter

Normal and demo actors map to admitted context or stable denial.

Domain

Existing business validation runs with `operationAdmission` available.
