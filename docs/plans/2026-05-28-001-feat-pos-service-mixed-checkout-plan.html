Summary

Add a mixed POS checkout that lets a cashier sell service work and retail product add-ons in one local-first register flow. Services remain service cases, product add-ons stay on the POS product sale path, and internal service material usage stays out of this release.

Core invariant: one cashier checkout, two correct business records, and one drawer tender event.

Requirements

  • POS can add service catalog entries alongside product lines.
  • Service lines create or attach to first-class service cases instead of fake SKUs.
  • Customer attribution is required before checkout when services are present.
  • One payment flow splits value across retail POS and service case targets.
  • Drawer/register expected money is affected once by the tender.
  • Internal service material usage remains outside POS v1.
  • Completion remains local-first and syncs through the existing local POS pipeline.
  • Active service catalog entries are snapshotted locally so service checkout works after reload while offline.

System-Wide Impact

  • POS register: gains service lines while product checkout remains the hot path.
  • Offline service catalog: active service rows are refreshed opportunistically and read from local storage first.
  • Local sync: service lines become part of the local completed-sale event and projection contract.
  • Service Ops: service cases receive POS-originated service payments and receipt links.
  • Inventory: retail add-ons decrement through POS; service materials remain in Service Ops.
  • Cash controls: drawer tender is counted once even when allocations split.
  • Reporting: service and retail totals must be explicit to avoid changing existing retail semantics silently.
