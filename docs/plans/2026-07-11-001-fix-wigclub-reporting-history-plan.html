Summary

Add a versioned, store-scoped interpretation policy used only by historical reporting maintenance. It allows approved Wigclub pre-schedule and currency-absent records to become auditable facts while preserving operational sources, unknown cost, strict matching, and preview/apply parity.

Release boundary: dev only. No production deploy, current-inventory activation, operational-source rewrite, inferred cost, or claim of transaction-wide fact rollback.

Problem and scope

The Wigclub preview produced 1,726 candidate facts: three eligible, 1,691 quarantined, and 32 payment conflicts. The plan addresses missing historical operating-period and currency evidence without treating today's configuration as historical truth.

Included

  • Dual-controlled immutable Wigclub policy
  • Reporting-owned historical period lineage
  • Policy-authorized GHS revenue currency
  • Exact canonical overlap
  • Sealed internal candidate manifest
  • Dev rebuild, activation, rollback proof

Excluded

  • Operational or Store Schedule mutation
  • Cost amount, valuation denomination, COGS, or profit inference
  • Global fingerprint weakening
  • Inventory authority cutover
  • Production deployment

Key decisions

  • Backfill-only policy: reporting-owned periods and GHS revenue inference never enter ordinary Store Schedule or live-ingress behavior.
  • End-to-end lineage: exactly-one Store Schedule or reporting-policy lineage is material in facts, fingerprints, projections, reconciliation, aggregation, and public contracts.
  • Dual control: distinct authenticated full admins create and approve immutable versions; trusted automation executes them.
  • Candidate and fact provenance: policy hash, inferred-field markers, original absence, period lineage, and audits survive independently of the run.
  • Sealed two-pass apply: bounded preflight stores immutable sanitized candidate semantics; write batches consume only the sealed manifest.
  • Strict matching: absent source currency is tolerated only under policy and exact candidate-known canonical semantic equality.
  • Unknown stays unknown: no cost amount, valuation denomination, COGS, or profit is inferred.
  • Internal lifecycle: transient manifests have terminal states and bounded retention; durable fact evidence survives cleanup.
  • Honest rollback: compare-and-swap baseline and replacement projections can switch and roll back; applied facts are not globally transactional.

Implementation units

U1 Policy and provenance

Persist authorized immutable policy, candidate markers, fact-linked evidence, approval, hashes, and inference counts.

U2 End-to-end period lineage

Carry reviewed reporting-owned lineage through facts, fingerprints, projections, reconciliation, and public contracts without changing shared Store Schedule behavior.

U3 Backfill normalization

Resolve only absent approved period/currency evidence across all source lanes with strict cost and identity boundaries.

U4 Sealed preview/apply

Materialize and seal immutable candidate semantics before writes, then apply only from the manifest with exact parity.

U5 Activation guard

Land the verified reconciliation-finalize compatibility fix without relaxing other activation checks.

U6 Dev proof

Preview, apply, establish baselines, activate replacements, smoke, roll back, reactivate, and record sanitized evidence.

Dev signoff gates

  1. Dual-control approved policy; zero unresolved reporting-period or revenue-currency blockers.
  2. Zero semantic conflicts; undenominated cost remains explicitly unknown.
  3. Late-page drift prevents manifest sealing with zero fact writes.
  4. Post-seal source changes cannot alter approved candidate semantics.
  5. Exact preview/manifest/apply outcomes by source domain.
  6. Candidate and fact-linked policy provenance survives independently; transient cleanup cannot delete it.
  7. Verified store_day and sku_day baseline and replacement generations with clean health.
  8. Compare-and-swap public reads switch across activation, rollback, and reactivation.
  9. No operational, Store Schedule, or inventory-authority change.
