Store service principals, with POS as the first consumer

Replace Athena's global human-shaped POS account with a reusable, store-scoped non-human identity foundation. Keep POS credentials and terminal proof in a consumer adapter, then migrate store by store without leaving a legacy bypass.

The boundary this plan establishes

1 store

Every principal, grant, binding, and session has one server-owned organization/store scope.

Default deny

A principal exists without authority until a closed-catalog capability is explicitly granted.

POS first

The foundation has no recovery-code or terminal semantics; POS contributes those through its adapter.

In scope

  • Generic principal, grant, auth-binding, session, and audit lifecycle
  • Store create/delete reconciliation and lifecycle hooks
  • Full-admin terminal enrollment
  • Two-phase terminal + credential recovery handoff
  • Server-boundary authorization inventory
  • Signed offline validation receipt and reconnect gate
  • Store-cohort migration and shared-account retirement

Outside this delivery

  • Second consumer
  • Cross-store and organization-wide principals
  • OAuth, API keys, and third-party developer identity
  • QR or recovery-code terminal enrollment
  • Staff, manager, drawer, and command redesign
  • Asymmetric browser key proof
  • Unrelated storefront Vite configuration

Authority stays in Athena domain state

Transport

Convex Auth identity

Establishes who presented the session. It does not carry current store authorization.

Foundation

Principal + explicit grant

Durable, store-scoped, independently revocable, and never represented as a human membership.

Consumer

POS session binding

Requires the store credential and active terminal proof while preserving staff and command gates.

Central invariant: every protected POS call reloads current session, principal, current store existence/available lifecycle state, pos.application grant, credential revision, and terminal binding. A still-valid bearer token cannot override revocation.

Widen, prove, enforce, retire

1 · Shadow

Deploy generic tables, store reconciliation hooks, actor resolver, and POS adapter without changing store or human outcomes.

2 · Reconcile

Preview/apply one principal, POS grant, and credential link per store; require one valid binding or explicit disposition per retained terminal. Auth bindings arise only from activated recovery exchanges.

3 · Recover

Enable selected cohorts and record each terminal's first successful recovery against its new principal.

4 · Enforce

Cut over only stores with complete census proof. Enforced stores never silently fall back.

5 · Retire

Stop shared issuance, invalidate legacy sessions, and disable the global account after fleet coverage.

6 · Narrow

Remove posAccountId, global email lookup, plaintext fields, local account constants, and compatibility branches.

Rollback boundary: before global retirement, an operator may explicitly return a store to its recorded compatibility mode. After retirement, rollback fixes or pauses the new path; it never recreates shared authority.

Material risks and controls

Privilege confusion

Mutually exclusive actor guards prevent service identities from falling through to email, membership, admin, or shared-demo policy.

Stale bearer authority

Exact Auth-session binding, an immutable principal transport user, two-phase activation, short service sessions, and durable current-state guards make revocation effective on the next server-backed call.

Credential leakage

Remove persisted plaintext, use versioned slow verification and lane throttling, normalize public failures, and keep audit secret-free.

Migration bypass

Compatibility is explicit and per-store; census gates and terminal recovery evidence precede enforcement and global retirement.

Offline revocation gap

Bound the local receipt and require reconnect validation before upload; preserve queued business evidence if denied, then route the terminal to administrator reconnection.

Terminal proof theft

Treat the current secret as bearer proof, rotate it safely, never log it, and avoid claims of hardware binding.

Final signoff gates

  • Every existing store has exactly one active POS-capable principal, linked credential, and migration state; every retained terminal has exactly one current principal/terminal/consumer binding or explicit disposition.
  • Every retained terminal has recovered against the new principal or is explicitly revoked/dispositioned.
  • Cross-store, unregistered, revoked, duplicate, and missing-capability cases share generic denial and create no partial session; terminal revocation preserves queued local records, leaves sibling terminals active, and offers only administrator reconnection.
  • Human, shared-demo, service-principal, terminal, staff, manager, drawer, and command authority remain distinct in code and audit.
  • No production reference to pos@wigclub.store, POS_APP_ACCOUNT_ID_KEY, the synthetic account's memberships, pos_only as machine authority, or persisted recovery plaintext remains after narrowing; legitimate human pos_only members remain supported.
  • The canonical Markdown plan carries exact paths, tests, migration gates, risks, and R/A/F/AE traceability.
