Outcome and scope

Public acquisition

/ becomes Athena's prospect-first product page. Existing customers keep a secondary sign-in path, and authenticated workspace dispatch moves to /app.

Truthful product proof

Store Pulse semantics, sanitized fixtures, and audited static media communicate sales standing, Athena-recorded history that compounds through use, product movement, and stock pressure without protected reads.

Durable conversion

A route-backed walkthrough form uses Athena's owned HTTP ingress to store prospect intent before notification, with idempotent retry, retained corrections, bounded abuse controls, and a PII lifecycle.

Evidence gate

Exactly five representative owners evaluate the positioning. Four must explain the problem, Athena's role, and its connected-record difference before final approval.

Excluded: protected public queries, live customer metrics, CRM or scheduler integration, pricing, self-service onboarding, automated replenishment, forecasting changes, and broad authenticated-shell refactors.

Key technical decisions

Public and operational entry are separate. Public rendering does not wait for auth or organization resolution; authenticated dispatch remains explicit at /app.
Real product, public-safe evidence. Reuse Store Pulse semantics through sanitized fixtures and audited fallbacks rather than exposing store-scoped queries.
Persistence defines success. A walkthrough is accepted when its Convex record is durable or the unchanged submission key is safely deduplicated; notification failure stays observable and retryable behind the boundary.
Replay memory stays bounded. Same-key equivalents remain idempotently accepted. Equivalent content under a fresh key receives a minimal HMAC replay alias while per-email and global capacity remains; exhaustion returns generic recoverable unavailable without persistence.
Anonymous writes use owned ingress. Origin, content type, and body size are checked before an internal Convex mutation; persistence and recovery functions are never public.
Retries are idempotent without losing corrections. The same key and payload dedupe; conflicting key reuse has no side effect; materially changed same-email requests persist as linked follow-ups.
PII has an owner and expiry. Internal-only open/resolved/abandoned operations, bounded notification attempts, stored-email proof for redaction, MailerSend field allowlists, and versioned server-keyed replay tombstones keep durable leads from becoming durable orphans.
Mobile stages evidence. Dense product surfaces become ordered, legible details rather than scaled-down dashboards.

System shape

Public visitor enters the public route shell
Product page shows sanitized Athena proof
Prospect requests a walkthrough or customer signs in
Owned HTTP ingress validates the anonymous request
Internal Convex command creates a durable lead
Leased notification workflow runs as a secondary side effect

The hero and conversion actions remain usable when rich media fails. The browser emits page view, CTA selection, and form start through the bounded funnel ingress; durable acceptance is appended only by the internal walkthrough transaction. Walkthrough success reflects persistence, not notification delivery.

Implementation-unit digest

  1. Positioning and proof gate
    External dependency: five eligible owner-operators and product-owner review.
    Pre-register the comprehension rubric, record every eligible response, map the core sequence plus one cross-channel and one small-team-control proof, maintain a freshness manifest, and pre-register the launch-review rubric.
  2. Public and app route separation
    Runs in parallel with units 1 and 3.
    Make / public, move authenticated dispatch to /app, redirect /landing, suppress public operational chrome, and keep process-root providers stable.
  3. Walkthrough persistence
    Deployment requires an accountable recipient and operational owner.
    Add owned HTTP ingress, immutable records/follow-ups, canonical same-key idempotency, budget-bounded fresh-key replay aliases, evidence-based budgets, ambiguity-safe notification attempts, internal-only resolution, and retention/redaction.
  4. Walkthrough experience
    Depends on units 2 and 3.
    Build the accessible /walkthrough form, retained failure state, retry identity, confirmation, and secondary sign-in.
  5. Product-proof system
    Depends on unit 1.
    Create sanitized fixtures, audited assets, staged responsive evidence, static poster fallback, and reduced-motion parity.
  6. Product-page narrative
    Depends on units 1, 2, 4, and 5.
    Compose the approved sales-to-stock story, two evidenced breadth moments, fixed crawler metadata, dedicated privacy-safe funnel ingress, and prospect-first conversion hierarchy.
  7. Integration and launch verification
    Depends on units 3 through 6.
    Exercise anonymous, authenticated, backend, media, accessibility, mobile, and deep-link paths, then refresh generated artifacts and run merge-grade validation.

Principal risks

RiskMitigation
Research disproves the heroReopen the positioning requirements before product-media or copy approval.
Real surfaces do not prove a transitionNarrow the story; never fabricate connective evidence.
Media leaks protected dataUse a field-level proof audit and production-owned fixtures/assets.
Root migration breaks app navigationCentralize route destinations and cover public, app, login, legacy, and deep-link paths.
Low-friction spam or duplicate leadsBound inputs, origin/body checks, honeypot, per-email ceilings, indexed same-key idempotency, budget-bounded fresh-key aliases, and non-enumerating outcomes. Alias exhaustion is recoverable unavailable without persistence.
Distributed abuse rotates identitiesOrigin/body controls, global persistence and notification budgets, monitoring, emergency disable, and an explicit challenge-provider escalation threshold.
Notification fails or times out ambiguouslyPreserve durable lead success, lease attempts, record outcome unknown, and require deliberate authorized resolution instead of blind resend.
Lead PII becomes orphaned or over-retainedName an owner, expose bounded internal lifecycle commands, apply retention/redaction, and preserve only non-PII replay evidence.

Verification strategy

  • Focused Vitest coverage for route ownership, provider composition, landing semantics, media fallbacks, form states, and Convex persistence/notification.
  • Unauthenticated Playwright coverage at desktop and mobile sizes, including keyboard, reduced motion, media failure, and walkthrough acceptance.
  • Authenticated route and POS deep-link characterization before and after migration.
  • Public bundle inspection for protected query references, sensitive fixture content, metadata, and asset weight.
  • Ingress checks for origin, body size, content type, same-key collisions, concurrent retries, bounded fresh-key alias exhaustion, abuse budgets, emergency disable, and generic public errors.
  • Operational verification for lead ownership, notification ambiguity, PII retention/redaction, privacy disclosure, and authorized recovery.
  • Privacy-safe funnel reconciliation for page view, CTA selection, form start, durable acceptance, and qualified owner follow-up.
  • A pre-registered first-week rubric separates message evidence from traffic quality, form/ingress incidents, notification failures, and owner follow-up latency; inconclusive evidence stays inconclusive.
  • Raw-response verification for canonical/Open Graph metadata and the product-specific walkthrough noindex rule; broad security-header hardening is tracked separately.
  • Generated route/API artifacts and Graphify refreshed before the full Athena PR gate.
